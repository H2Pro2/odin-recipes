Pizza

Description

This is another one from Italy. It's easy to make but hard to master.

I'm going to assume everyone knows and loves the pizza. So I am just going to

push you in the right direction of finding out what you like most about your specific

type of your pizza.

Ingredients

Dough

  • 300g of type 00 flour
  • ~250ml of water
  • 3g of dry yeast
  • 1 1/2 tsp of salt
Preparation

Mix the flour with the salt and yeast in a bowl. Now add most of the water and mix it.

If there is still a lot of flour in the bowl add little by little from your 250ml bottle of water.

At some point your dough is hydrated enough to form a homogenous dough ball.

If it feels dry, moisten your hands. If it feels wet add a bit of water.

Now you just got to kneed it for around 10 minutes.At this point you can take out your

dough ball and add a little olive oil to the bowl. Put the dough ball back in and cover

the bowl with something so your dough doesn't dry out.

Now you have to wait for atleat 2 hours.

Marinara

  • 1/2 can of peeled tomatoes
  • 2 - 3 tsp salt
Preperation

Crush the peeled tomatoes in a bowl and add the salt. That's pretty much it.

Baking the Pizza in a homeoven

Since I'm sadly not one of the people with an ooni koda, I had to somehow find a way to

make a pizza in a homeoven. But first we have to form our dough ball.

Place the ball on a lightly floured workplace, then push it down with the palm of your hand.

Try to flatten it as much as possible while leaving something that resembles the outer crust.

Now hold up the outer crust with both of your hands like it is a steering wheel and

turn it while the dough doesn't touch the workplace.

Move your hands along the crust till gravity did it's job and you have your desired thickness.

Now place it on a baking sheet which is ontop of a baking iron.

Spread the marinara on the pizza and you're nearly done!

Preheat the oven to 250Â°C and wait till it's burning hot.

Now place the pizza on the lowest place in your oven for 9 minutes.

After that open the oven, and place some cut pieces of fresh mozzarella over it.

Right after that place the pizza on the topmost place in the oven and wait till the crust

starts to get to dark for your liking.

To finish it all off add a bit of fresh basel and some olive oil.

You're now left with a pretty decent homeoven version of a neapolitan pizza!

Enjoy!
